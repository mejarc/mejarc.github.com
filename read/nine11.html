9/11

"911," oil on canvas paper.

...Bink and I watched the most staggering piece of footage I've ever seen. Its incredibility paradoxically brought to me a more solid understanding of the day's significance.
The video was the work of a camera crew which had somehow scrambled into place after the first World Trade Center impact. Intended to capture images of the billowing smoke, the camera filmed instead a jet airliner vanishing into the second World Trade Center tower. As I watched I wanted to think that I was seeing The Matrix again, and that this plane would emerge whole on the other side of the building, having rearranged the molecules of the skyscraper's glass windows to some readily repaired plasma.
I watched the news show's rerun of the video perhaps three or four times in fifteen minutes, and each time I had managed to forget the ending.
